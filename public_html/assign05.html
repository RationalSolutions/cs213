So many dice!

When it comes to playing Dungeons & Dragons, the only thing that you will need to purchase in order to play is a set of dice. This isn't your standard set of two cube dice that you are used to throwing when you are playing Monopoly or Risk, this set of 7 dice will help determine the fate of your character and the results of their actions. Each of these have a unique shape, name and are generally used for a wide variety of purposes in the game.

D20 - Icosahedron (20 sides)

When you think of D&D and dice this is probably what most people think of. This is used when you want to know if something you tried to do was successful. You have a 5% chance to land on a "natural" 20 and score a critical hit!

D12 - Dodecahedron (12 sides)

This is one of the least common used dice in D&D as it is most commonly used to roll damage on the largest weapons.

D10 - Pentagonal Trapezohedron (10 sides)

The set of D&D dice contains not one but TWO d10 dice. The faces of one are numbered 0 to 9, and the faces of the other go from 00 to 90 by tens. This is used for mainly for rolling damage but using the two together allows you to roll what is called a percentile roll. To make a 'percentile' roll, you roll both die and add them together, with one exception: a double zero roll is 100.

D8 - Octahedron (8 sides)

Often used to roll damage done by large weapons.

D6 - Cube (6 sides)

Your common run of the mill die. This is used for everything from rolling the starting stats on your character to rolling the damage from a wizards fireball.

D4 - Tetrahedron (4 sides)

This is the one die that is more dangerous outside of the game than inside of the game, mainly because it is more violent to the unprotected foot than legos ever were. Commonly used to determine the damage caused by small weapons.
