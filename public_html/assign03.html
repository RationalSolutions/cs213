Family Time and Slaying Dragons

One of the things that I end up talking about when I am not busy working as a Software QA/QE Engineer or doing homework for one of my classes is usually Dungeons & Dragons. Prior to 2016 I only knew about it from proximity in the nerd/geek culture, at that time my youngest child was turning 3 and I was starting to feel that my children were becoming zombies to electronics and were lacking in imagination and problem solving skills. I was inspired when I read this article from www.lifehacker.com and all the concerns that I was having were answered by the various aspects of this article. From there I started going to my local game store so that I could learn how to play the game and see if it was really all about satanic rituals and witchcraft. Luckily for my children I was unable to detect any evil and I have been running as the dungeon master at least one session of D&D a week for the past several years.
